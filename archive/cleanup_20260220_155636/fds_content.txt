 2. System Description  
 The IAS remotely controls the LNG re -liquefaction plant. Monitoring and control are performed from the 
operator station i n the cargo control room (CCR).  
However, operating responsibilities can be transferred to the oth er IAS operator stations in the 
wheelhouse or engine control room (ECR) when required. All deck piping instruments (for pressure, 
temperature, flow) and valve position indicators are interfaced to the IAS by remote input/output units 
approved for installat ion in hazardous areas.  
Process control stations for control of N2 compander, heaters and heat exchagers are installed in the 
electrical equipment room (EER).  
The ove rall design of the Hi -ERS(N)  is to maintain the cargo tank pressure for LNGc by re -lique fying 
surplus BOG calculated by GMS. When the Hi -ERS(N) is operated, the surplus BOG after supply to gas 
consumer including main engine, DFGE and GCU by LDC is fully liquefied by reverse Brayton cycle as 
sub-cooled condition in Nitrogen Heat Exchanger and then returns to cargo tank.  
The maker of the re-liquefaction  equipment describes how the equipment is designed and should be 
operated. On this vessel the maker is Cryostar  and Donghwa Pneutec . To allow remote operation of the 
systems, all control of valves , heater, heat exchangers, N2 boosting compressor and N2 comp ander  
(including anti surge and N2 compander and compressor monitoring) is integrated in the IAS system.

 3.1.2.  Control  
3.1.2.1.  Load control of ERS(N)  
Re-liquefaction load of Hi -ERS(N)  is controlled in either “Tank pressure control” mode or “BOG Flow control” 
mode. If tank pressure control mode is  selected re -liquefaction load is automatically controlled according to 
output of tank pressure controller from GMS as remote set point, SP GMS (Max. Output 1,500 kg/h). If BOG 
flow control mode is selected re -liquefaction load is set by operator in manual.   
 
The GMS calculates the amount of BOG remaining after supply fuel to engines and the surplus BOG is set to 
the maximum output limit of the tank pressure controller for Hi -ERS(N).  
 
The output of tank pressure controller is sent to the re -liquefaction loa d controller as remote set point (SP GMS). 
The re -liquefaction load controller is provided to control the re -liquefaction flow rate and load of N2 refrigerant 
loop. The re -liquefaction flow rate is controlled by BOG flow cont roller (71FIC 001), and load of N 2 refrigerant 
loop is controlled by N2 compander suc tion pressure controller (81PIC 003). The pressure of the nitrogen loop 
is controlled to have the correct mass flowrate of the compander corresponding to the correct cooling capacity 
of Hi -ERS(N) .  
 
The re-liquefaction load controller determines the set point of BOG flow controller as remote set point (SP REL). 
When SP GMS is higher than SP REL, load increase of Hi-ERS(N) is required. On the contrary, when SP GMS is 
lowe r than SP REL, load decre ase  is re quired. The  BOG flow  rate  should be  de pendently controlled with the 
nitrogen refrigerant loop in order to maintain the temperature of condensate BOG (T C) within control range -
156°C ~ - 159°C. If the temperature of condensate BOG (T C) is colder than control range (Level L) at the given 
BOG flow rate, it means that the nitrogen refrigerant mass flow is too higher than required. On the contrary, if 
the condensate BOG temperature (T C) is warmer than control range (Level H), it means that the nitrogen 
refrigerant mass flow is insufficient. Therefore, nitrogen refrigerant mass flow is manipulated according to the 
BOG flow rate variation. SP REL is changed by 50 kg/h at a time when one of load up or down conditions is 
true. Cycle timer is started for 60 seconds (adjustable by administrator) since re-liquefaction load controller 
is activated. It is to pre vent furthe r load change  during the  time . Cycle  timer is use d when T C is controlled

 3.2. N2 compander  
The main function of the N2 compander is to compress and e xpand the nitrogen and to supply the nitrogen to 
N2 heat exchanger in order to re -liquefy surplus BOG.  
 
The nitrogen is first compressed by a radial centrifugal compressor and then cooled -down by passing through 
a watercooler, where the heat of compression  is rejected. This is successively repeated twice, resulting in a 
three stage compression with intermediate cooling by fresh water.  
Inside the turbine the nitrogen is expanded to a low pressure.  
The turbine and the three compressors stages are integrated  on each side of a single speed shafts mounted on 
a common gearbox, forming a machine configuration called compander.  
The compander is operated at a fixed speed.

 3.2.1.2.  Compander Anti surge control  
 
 
Each compressor fitted on compander is protected by its dedicated A/S controller by recycling gas i n case of 
too low flow. Only one recycling valve 51FCV 10 is provided for the three compression stages. Surge points 
are programmed into the controllers in order to generate a Control line Setpoint corresponding to minimum 
required flow of each compression stage (the A/S controllers set point can not be modified by operators).  
 
- First compressor is protected by 51XIC 1 
- Second stage by 51XIC 2, and  
- Third stage by 51XIC 3. 
 
The surge controller is the ultimate protect ion considering the controller 51 FRIC0 41 which should keep 
compressors near design point by controlling compression stages at design flowrate.  
 
Anyway, a cause of approach of surge could be the unexpected diminution of th e process controller 51FRIC0 41 
output. Consequently, in order to decouple  the effect of the A/S controllers 51XIC 1/2/3 and controller 
51FRIC041, the controller 51FRIC0 41 output is frozen as soon one A/S controller output becomes higher than 
10% during 10s. (activated after start -up and de -activated at stop)  
 
One minute later th e controller 51 FRIC041 is switched back in AUTO to its previous set point following a 
ramp of 0.1 / min (to be confirmed later).  
 
At reduced speed, the first stage and second compressor stages are protected with a dedicated con trol valve 
(51SCV 1/2). These  valves are open at 100% until the speed -up to full speed of the compander. In order to 
avoid interference between A/S controllers when these valves are fully open, the safety function and safety 
trip are not active an d the anti surge controllers 51XIC 1& 2  are forced in Man 0% to inhibit the action of these

 3.2.1.2.4 . N2 Compander A/S controllers override (51HIC10)  
 
Compander A/S va lve 51FCV 10 can be operated independen tly from the A/S controllers 51XIC 1/2/3, by using 
the ma nual override controller 51HIC 10.  
 
This HIC function allows to open the A/S valve more than the A/S controllers would ask for, but the reverse 
is not possible. The HIC output is added t o the A/S controllers output 51XIC1/2/3 in a sum function 51XY 10. 
 
 For a 100% HIC output signal, the A/S va lve is fully open. The valve 51FCV 10 is f itted with external 
enable/disable  function 51HIC 10A that can be switched in external disable  for maintenance only. I f the HIC 
of the valve is in external disable , the solenoid has to be energized. This function is used to allow the tuning 
of the valve during maintenance. When the compande r is in remote, the function 51HIC10  is automatically 
switched in external enable .

 3.3.4.  Sequence  
Start/stop location (LOP, starter panel, IAS) can be selected on the IAS. Only one location from among LOP, 
starter panel, and IAS shall be selected.  
 
When LOP  is selected in the IAS, N2 booster system(N2 Boosting compressor & N2 dryer) can be 
started/stopped in LOP. When starter panel is selected in the IAS, N2 booster system(N2 Boosting compressor 
& N2 dryer) can be started/stopped in starter panel.  
 
When IAS  is selected in the IAS, N2 booster system(N2 Boosting compressor & N2 dryer) can be 
started/stopped in IAS. Can not be changed the start location during the operation.  
 
In Auto mode the start sequence is activated  automatically in the following cases (OR)  : 
-81XV0002 fully opened  
-82PIT0002 < 7.5 bar  
-82PIT0001 < 26 bar  
 
In Auto mode the stop sequence is activated automatically in the following cases(AND) :  
-81XV0002 fully closed  
-82PIT0002 > 9.0 bar  
-82PIT0001 > 29.5 bar  
-N2 Commander(Stopped mode)  and XV110A fully c losed  
 
 
EM’CY stop is possible regardless of mode.  
 
Time control mode and temperature control mode can be selected on IAS. Only one mode shall be selected.  
 
Before starting the N2 booster system, the following setting value shall be entered in IAS.  
 -
 Local / remote mode selection
- Control mode selection (Time control mode or temperature control mode)
- SP01 : Set point of loading pressure (29bar.g)
- SP02 : Set point of unloading pressure (30bar.g)
- SP03 : Set point of heater on temperature (210 ℃)
- SP04 : Set point of heater off temperature (230 ℃)
- SP05 : Set point of dew point (-80 ℃ at ATM / -57 ℃ at 29bar)
- SP07 : Set point of heating time (9,000 sec)
- SP10 : Set point of cooling complete temperature
- Alarm & trip set point & time delay
